Questions & Answers

What's the difference between a compressor with a tank pressure gauge feature versus one without?

The tank pressure gauge is important because it allows you to monitor how much air is available in your compressor easily. Knowing how much air pressure you have helps you control your project and ensure you don't over- or under-inflate.

What specifications should I look for to handle bigger jobs like framing, roofing, and bolting?

Contractors completing framing nailing, roof nailing, or bolting often need a higher-power stationary air compressor. For the power to drive tools like framing nailers and impact wrenches, it is best to look at models with a larger tank (20 gallons or higher) and a higher air delivery rate (4 SCFM at 90 PSI or more). Models with higher air delivery rates and larger tank sizes will do the job!

I mainly use an air compressor to inflate tires and do basic home projects. Do I need a huge, expensive compressor?

A light-duty or consumer air compressor is likely perfect for inflating tires, powering nail guns for small projects, or even airbrushing. These are portable, affordable, and easy to use, making them ideal for light-duty tasks around the house without breaking the bank.
