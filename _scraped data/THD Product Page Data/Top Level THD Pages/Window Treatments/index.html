About Window Treatments

When considering the best window treatments for your home, we have lots of great options. Think of your window treatments the same way you would buy clothes for yourself. Everyone has their own style and preferences, and window treatments are no different. Do you go for fashion, functionality or a blend of both when picking your window treatments?

We offer a wide variety of blinds, blind parts, curtains, shades and other window treatments in a stylish assortment of materials and colors. Window treatments are not just simple window blinds and shades these days. You have choices when selecting your window treatments. We carry everything from black curtains and vertical blinds to sheer curtains, blackout shades and more.

Window Treatments That Let the Light In or Out

Window treatments are a great way to control the amount of light in a room. We have a large selection of window treatments that include room darkening shades, blackout curtains, outdoor curtains and white curtains.

Blinds are among the most popular window treatments. Check out our variety of wood blinds, vinyl mini blinds, cordless blinds, and blind parts.

If you've made the decision to install faux wood blinds for your window treatments, there are a couple of things to do before getting started. First, be sure to determine the type of mounting type you will need, either inside or outside. Next, choose the type of faux wood blind that best suits your home and style.

New window blinds can set a mood and highlight your decor, but there are also great window treatments to help you control light levels, give privacy and reduce your energy bills. Shades are window treatments that provide ways to create different looks with texture, pattern and fabric weight. You could even try layering window curtains over your roman shades for maximum privacy and style. If youre looking for room darkening shades or so-called blackout shades, we offer a solid selection of window panels thatll get the job done with a little more effectiveness than your standard window treatments.

Window treatments also provide privacy where you need it most, like in your bedroom or bathroom. If you are considering bathroom window treatments, make sure the material is moisture resistant, like a plastic or faux wood. Same for your kitchen window treatments, especially the over-the-sink window. Getting the right bay window treatments can be a challenge. Some of your options include dressing each window separately, mounting your window coverings on the inside of the molding or you can give plantation shutters a try.

There is no such thing as a standard size window, so make sure to measure yours first before buying window treatments. If you need or want custom window treatments, well be there to help every step of the way, from measuring to completion.
